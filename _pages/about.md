__I am a molecular ecologist at the University of Exeter with broad interests in epidemiology, host-microbe interactions and statistics.__  
I lead a team of researchers studying the interplay between microbiomes and animal life histories, with a particular focus on nutrition, reproduction and pathogen resistance. I'm also passionate about determining best practice for the application of complex statistical models to even more complex large datasets.  
<br>

__CURRENT RESEARCH PROJECTS__ 

1. The gut microbiome and host nutritional health in migratory birds
2. The amphibian skin microbiome and it's role in defending host from lethal pathogens including _Bd_ and Ranaviruses.
3. Using metagenomics to understand host contact networks and routes of pathogen transmission.
4. The Gut-Brain Axis and Host Ageing and Health, using _Drosophila_ as a model system & led by [Amanda Bretman at Leeds](https://biologicalsciences.leeds.ac.uk/school-of-biology/staff/36/dr-amanda-bretman)
